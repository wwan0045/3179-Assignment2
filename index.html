Where Is Your Next Tourist Destination?

At the start of 2020, COVID-19 decimated global tourism as countries struggled to prevent surges in infections and deaths. Despite Australians managing the spread of COVID-19 better than most countries, they too were heavily restricted on overseas tourism.

This report is intended to help Australians make a better decision on which country they might want to visit first once restrictions have fully lifted on outbound tourism. The presentation will focus on past tourism trends prior to COVID-19 on the basis of: tourism arrivals per country, where people are spending the most on tourism, safety of country through GPI (Global Peace Index) and affordability.

Where Did Tourists Travel The Most Before Covid-19?

From this world map in 2019, it is clear that the countries with the most tourist arrivals are the United States, China and a select few in the European Continent.

Where Did Tourists Spend The Most Money?

In total, countries in Europe have consistently received the most revenue from tourists since 1995.

In 2019, tourists spent a whopping 407.7 billion USD in Europe while travelling!

Where Are The Safest Countries?

Global Peace Index measures a country's safety. The lower the index, the safer the country.

60% of the top 10 countries ranked in the 2019 Global Peace Index are European countries.

If you want to travel to the safest European countries, you can travel to Slovenia, Switzerland, Austria, Portugal or Denmark.

Affordable Countries to Visit

Based on the 2019 data available, the list of 10 countries represent the lowest amount of money spent per tourist.
